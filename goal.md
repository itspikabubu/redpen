# Article Goal

## What this post should accomplish
Drive engagement on LinkedIn and get people to click through to the full FC blog post. Position Leo and Joanne as having a sharp, non-obvious thesis on how AI changes orgs — not just individual productivity. Make readers stop scrolling and engage (like, comment, repost).

## Key message
The real AI transformation isn't copilots making individuals faster — it's the entire org structure being redesigned around agentic systems. Companies that figure this out early win; companies that bolt AI onto old structures get left behind.

## Tone
Direct, confident, conversational. Short punchy lines. This is LinkedIn, not a white paper. Should feel like Leo talking to a smart friend, not broadcasting a press release.

## Target audience
Primary: tech executives, engineering leaders, VPs of Product, CTOs who are actively dealing with AI org questions. Secondary: founders building in the AI tooling/infra space, fellow VCs.

## Must include
- The opening hook question (strong, keep it)
- Specific enterprise data points (120→25, 0.75→0.1, "My First Pull Request")
- The "cars on dirt roads" analogy
- Clear CTA to read the full piece

## Must NOT include
- Generic AI hype language
- "The future is here" type platitudes
- Direct product pitches
- Buzzword soup

## Format
linkedin

## Stopping criteria
min_score >= 7.5 across all personas
