Are you going to manage agents, or are agents going to manage you?

That's the question Joanne Chen and I keep coming back to after speaking with executives from 25+ enterprises who are actively thinking about it.

We think the future of work isn't about individual productivity anymore. For the last two years, the conversation around AI has been about individual productivity. Give everyone Copilot. Ship faster. Write more code.

But the real shift is bigger than that. It's not about making people faster — it's about redesigning the entire org around these new agentic systems.

And the results are wild:
→ A 120-person engineering team is planning to cut down to 25
→ One company went from 0.75 engineers per microservice to 0.1 — a single engineer now owns 10 services
→ At a 1,000-person company, every PM, designer, and data scientist must ship code. They call the project "My First Pull Request"

Most companies are still in the "cars-on-dirt-roads" phase. The real unlock isn't the car — it's the highway system, the suburbs, the supply chains that come after.
